HOW TO PREPARE GARI SOAKINGS

Gari can be eaten with very little preparations as it is sold already cooked. It is a grainy fibrous carbohydrate that is made from dry frying grated cassava(tapioca) in an aluminium pan till it is fully cooked and it starts yellowing. It is eaten across West Africa and ingenious communities in the Amazon. Gari is very filling and as a dry fibre it softens and expands with water. This is why it is popular with secondary school boarding students. I love Gari Soakings because it is the easiest of gari recipes to make and you need less than three minutes preparation time to quench your hungar, all other recipes are eaten coupled with a stew or soup. The water in this recipe can be replaced with other fluids such as milk hence it is called soakings.
